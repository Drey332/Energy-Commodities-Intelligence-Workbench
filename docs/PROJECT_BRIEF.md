# Africa Energy & Commodities Intelligence Workbench

## Public-Data Monitoring For Development Knowledge Teams

### Project Summary

The Africa Energy & Commodities Intelligence Workbench is a local Streamlit
platform for monitoring public information about African energy, commodities,
finance, infrastructure, and risk. It combines live public sources, optional
API-key news and data sources, uploaded reports, CSV datasets, normalized
signals, event clustering, evidence-backed Q&A, and exportable intelligence
briefs.

The project is designed for the kind of work done by development data,
partnerships, research, and knowledge-management teams: tracking changes,
structuring messy information, reviewing evidence, and turning source material
into clear knowledge products.

### Problem

Development teams rarely work from one clean dataset. They work across reports,
news, partner updates, PDFs, policy documents, and spreadsheets. A single issue,
such as fuel-market reform in Nigeria or copper supply-chain investment in
Zambia and DRC, can involve many source types:

- official reports,
- media coverage,
- commodity and energy indicators,
- donor and IFI announcements,
- project documents,
- policy changes,
- risk and governance signals.

The challenge is not only finding information. The challenge is organizing it
into usable, source-transparent intelligence.

### Solution

The workbench provides a structured workflow:

1. Collect source material from uploaded files and public connectors.
2. Normalize raw items into common monitoring signals.
3. Classify each signal by country, region, sector, commodity, event type, tone,
   risk flags, source, and relevance.
4. Cluster related signals into developments.
5. Compare monitoring signals with report evidence and dataset summaries.
6. Generate briefs, review queues, source tables, and exports.

The platform keeps the analyst in the loop. It flags uncertain or high-risk
items for review before they are treated as reliable knowledge.

### Example Workflow

An analyst wants to understand recent developments in African energy and
commodities.

They run a monitoring cycle with a query such as:

```text
Africa energy oil gas mining copper cobalt power investment finance risk
```

The system collects available public signals, normalizes them, groups related
items, and shows:

- which countries appear most often,
- which commodities and sectors are active,
- what risk flags appear,
- which sources succeeded or failed,
- which items need review,
- which developments are suitable for a brief.

The analyst can then ask follow-up questions, promote important developments,
generate a short brief, and export the evidence.

### Why Africa Energy And Commodities Matter

Energy and commodities sit at the center of many development questions:

- electricity access and power reliability,
- oil and gas revenue and fiscal exposure,
- fuel-market reforms and inflation,
- mining governance and critical minerals,
- infrastructure investment,
- industrialization and local value addition,
- climate transition and resilience,
- jobs and private-sector development,
- debt, governance, and social-license risk.

A public-data intelligence platform can help development teams monitor these
issues more consistently and transparently.

Organizations such as UNDP, the World Bank, UNESCO, regional development banks,

public information into useful knowledge products. This project demonstrates a
practical version of that workflow using open data, local processing, source
transparency, and human review.

tool rather than a job-specific demo.
