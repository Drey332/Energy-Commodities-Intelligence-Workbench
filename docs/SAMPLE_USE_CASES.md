# Sample Use Cases

## 1. Nigeria Fuel-Market Monitoring

User question:

```text
What recent signals matter for Nigeria's oil, fuel, and energy sector?
```

Sources used:

- GDELT or optional news APIs for current news.
- World Bank or institutional reports for context.
- Uploaded policy PDFs or CSVs if available.
- Monitoring events already promoted by the analyst.

Output generated:

- normalized signals by country, sector, commodity, tone, risk flags, and event
  type,
- event clusters about fuel markets, pricing, investment, policy, or risk,
- review queue items for weak or high-risk evidence,
- a short monitoring brief with sources.

Why it matters:

Nigeria's fuel and oil sector affects fiscal revenue, imports, inflation,
energy access, foreign exchange pressure, and social risk.

## 2. DRC/Zambia Copper And Cobalt Monitoring

User question:

```text
What is changing around copper and cobalt in DRC and Zambia?
```

Sources used:

- live/keyless news connectors,
- optional API-key news connectors,
- EITI or institutional documents if ingested,
- uploaded commodity or project datasets.

Output generated:

- country/commodity signal table,
- event clusters around mining, investment, governance, exports, or disputes,
- risk flags for governance, environmental risk, conflict, corruption, or social
  license,
- evidence-backed Q&A and brief export.

Why it matters:

Copper and cobalt are central to export revenue, industrial value chains,
critical minerals strategy, infrastructure demand, and governance risk.

## 3. South Africa Electricity And Power Risk Monitoring

User question:

```text
Which South Africa power-sector developments should an analyst monitor?
```

Sources used:

- news connectors,
- World Bank documents,
- uploaded power-sector reports,
- structured datasets with electricity or energy-access indicators.

Output generated:

- signals classified under power, electricity, infrastructure, investment, and
  risk,
- event clusters about reliability, investment, grid constraints, reform, or
  project delays,
- dataset profile charts when CSV indicators are available,
- monitoring supervisor summary and action queue.

Why it matters:

Power reliability shapes growth, investment, household welfare, fiscal exposure,
and transition planning.

## 4. Africa Energy Access And Development Indicator Context

User question:

```text
How do uploaded energy-access or development datasets compare across countries?
```

Sources used:

- uploaded CSV datasets,
- World Bank indicator connector results,
- institutional reports used as context.

Output generated:

- dataset insight with row and column counts,
- column typing and missingness,
- numeric distributions and rankings,
- top and bottom values,
- chart-ready summaries,
- evidence-backed explanation.

Why it matters:

Energy access and infrastructure indicators help analysts identify gaps,
country patterns, and where further source review is needed.

## 5. Weekly Intelligence Brief Generation

User question:

```text
Create a weekly Africa energy and commodities monitoring brief.
```

Sources used:

- current monitoring-cycle signals,
- promoted/stored monitoring events,
- uploaded reports and datasets,
- source registry and audit records.

Output generated:

- headline and key developments,
- affected countries and commodities,
- risks and opportunities,
- source-status summary,
- evidence table,
- review queue,
- Markdown, CSV, PDF, JSON, and ZIP exports.

Why it matters:

Development teams need recurring monitoring products that are short enough to
read, but still traceable to source evidence.
